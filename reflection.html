My Honest Reflection

Reflecting on my dream company, the value I can offer, my genuine strengths and weaknesses, and why I believe I am a strong candidate for a cybersecurity career.

Dream Company

🔐
Target Organisation

Nexagate Malaysia

Leading Malaysian cybersecurity company specialising in managed security services, SOC operations, and enterprise threat defence

Private Sector Managed SOC Threat Intelligence Kuala Lumpur, MY Enterprise Security

Reflection Questions

Why do you want to work for this company?
I want to work for Nexagate Malaysia because it is one of the most respected homegrown cybersecurity companies in Malaysia, with a strong focus on managed security services and SOC operations. What draws me to Nexagate specifically is that they operate a real, active SOC environment — meaning I would be working on genuine threat monitoring and incident response from day one, not just shadowing or doing administrative tasks. As a Malaysian company, Nexagate understands the local threat landscape, industry regulations such as PDPA, and the specific challenges faced by Malaysian enterprises. That local context matters to me because I want to build a career that is meaningful and relevant to my own country's digital security future. Nexagate also appears to invest in developing talent, which aligns with my ambition to grow steadily from a Tier 1 analyst toward a specialised security role.
What value can you bring to this company?
I bring a solid academic foundation in networking and cybersecurity paired with genuine enthusiasm for the work itself. My hands-on experience with Wireshark, Nmap, Linux, and network protocol analysis means I can contribute meaningfully to monitoring and triage tasks without a long ramp-up period. I am a self-directed learner — I use TryHackMe, Cisco NetAcad, and online resources outside of class because the subject genuinely interests me. This means Nexagate would be investing in someone who will keep growing independently. I am also detail-oriented and methodical, which matters enormously in a SOC environment where missing a single indicator of compromise can have serious consequences. Additionally, being bilingual in Malay and English allows me to communicate findings clearly to both technical teams and non-technical stakeholders within a Malaysian business context.
Why should we hire you?
You should hire me because I have chosen this career path deliberately and intentionally — not by accident. Everything I am studying, every certification I am pursuing, and every project I complete is pointed at becoming an effective SOC Analyst. I understand my current skills gap honestly and I am actively closing it. I am coachable, I take feedback well, and I approach security work with a sense of real responsibility rather than just technical curiosity. Nexagate would be getting someone who is motivated not just by salary but by a genuine desire to protect people and organisations from threats that can cause real harm. I am also at an early stage of my career, which means I bring fresh perspectives and I am fully shaped by current tools and practices — not outdated habits from a decade ago.
What skills make you suitable for this role?
My core strength is my networking knowledge — TCP/IP, the OSI model, protocol behaviour, and traffic analysis — which is the foundation of effective SOC work. Threats travel over networks, and understanding how legitimate traffic looks is what makes anomalies visible. My Linux proficiency puts me at ease in the command-line environment that most SOC tools run on. My Wireshark experience gives me a head start on packet-level investigation. Beyond technical skills, I bring analytical thinking and attention to detail — qualities that matter when you are triaging dozens of alerts and must distinguish a real threat from a false positive quickly and accurately. My written communication skills also mean I can produce clear, structured incident reports that are useful to both technical and management audiences.
Why would a company choose to hire you over others?
Because I combine academic grounding, self-directed learning, and honest self-awareness that many candidates at my stage lack. I do not oversell what I know — I have mapped my current skills against what a SOC Analyst actually needs and I have a clear, structured plan to close every gap. That kind of professional maturity is rare in a fresh graduate. I am also someone who finds genuine satisfaction in the detail-heavy, sometimes repetitive work of alert triage and log analysis — many people want the exciting parts of cybersecurity without being willing to do the foundational work that makes a SOC function. I am willing to do that work, do it carefully, and improve continuously. For Nexagate, hiring me means investing in someone who will still be there and growing five years from now.
