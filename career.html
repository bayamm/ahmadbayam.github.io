What Is a SOC Analyst?

A SOC Analyst is a cybersecurity professional who works within a Security Operations Centre to monitor, detect, investigate, and respond to cybersecurity threats and incidents in real time. They act as the frontline defenders of an organisation's digital assets, constantly watching network traffic, logs, and alerts generated by security tools.

SOC Analysts operate in tiers — Tier 1 handles alert triage and initial investigation, Tier 2 performs deeper analysis and containment, and Tier 3 handles advanced threat hunting and forensics. My initial goal is to enter as a Tier 1 SOC Analyst and progress upward.

Job Responsibilities

👁

Continuous Monitoring

Monitor security alerts and events 24/7 using SIEM tools like Splunk and IBM QRadar. Review dashboards for anomalies and suspicious activity.

🔎

Alert Triage & Analysis

Investigate security alerts to determine whether they are true positives or false positives. Prioritise incidents based on severity and potential impact.

🚨

Incident Response

Follow incident response playbooks to contain, eradicate, and recover from security incidents. Document findings and escalate as needed.

🌐

Network Traffic Analysis

Analyse network packets and logs using tools like Wireshark to identify malicious communication patterns, lateral movement, or data exfiltration.

📋

Reporting & Documentation

Produce clear incident reports for management and technical teams. Maintain logs and track recurring patterns over time.

🛡

Threat Intelligence

Consume threat intelligence feeds to stay updated on new attack techniques, indicators of compromise (IOCs), and adversary behaviours.

Relevance to Modern Cybersecurity

🔗

Rising Threat Landscape

Ransomware, phishing, and APT attacks are increasing globally. SOC Analysts are the first line of defence, essential for detecting and stopping these attacks before they cause major damage.

🏛

Regulatory Compliance

Organisations in Malaysia must comply with PDPA, and globally with standards like ISO 27001 and PCI-DSS. SOC teams help organisations maintain compliance by monitoring and documenting security events.

☁

Cloud & Hybrid Environment Security

As companies migrate to the cloud, the attack surface expands. SOC Analysts now need cloud-native security skills to monitor AWS, Azure, and GCP environments alongside traditional infrastructure.

🤖

AI-Augmented Security Operations

Modern SOCs leverage AI and machine learning to process millions of alerts. SOC Analysts need to work alongside these tools, validating AI-generated alerts and making critical human judgement calls.
